# Video Design: More Code Is Not Progress

## Style Prompt

An editorial technical documentary about an AI-agent product foundry control plane. The visual language should feel like a serious paper, a terminal, and an investment committee memo converging into one live system. Use flowing camera motion, code/report surfaces, animated decision paths, and restrained data emphasis. It must not feel like a slide deck or startup promo.

## Colors

- Paper: `#f6f1e8`
- Ink: `#151515`
- Muted ink: `#5f615d`
- Ready green: `#6fa889`
- Proof amber: `#c79842`
- No-winner red: `#b85f56`
- Panel: `#fffaf1`
- Rule line: `#d8cdbb`

## Typography

- Thesis/title: Georgia, `Times New Roman`, serif
- Interface/body: Inter, Avenir, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif
- Code/data: SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Monaco, Consolas, monospace

## Motion

- Continuous motion over discrete slides.
- Use camera pans, scale reveals, line tracing, row-by-row evidence accumulation, and state transitions.
- Prefer `power3.out` entrances, `power2.inOut` camera motion, and soft staggered reveals.
- Animate transforms and opacity only.

## Audio

- Narration should use Kokoro-ONNX with a calm technical voice.
- Background music should be low, procedural, and documentary-like: steady enough to give the artifact shape, never loud enough to compete with the voice.
- Avoid upbeat corporate beds, synthetic hype stingers, and dramatic trailer impacts.

## What Not To Do

- No PowerPoint-like title cards.
- No generic AI robot imagery.
- No neon cyberpunk palette.
- No hype framing about "20 AI startups."
- No dense full-transcript captions.
