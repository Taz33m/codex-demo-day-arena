# Final Demo Day Report

## Final Status

The Codex Demo Day arena is closed.

No owner agents should be launched. No product features should be added. No new phase should be created.

The arena produced 20 standalone product-shaped repos, validated them technically, ran buyer-usefulness review, and then reached the correct stopping point: the remaining bottleneck is external proof, not local development.

## Portfolio Process

The arena began as a 20-team Demo Day simulation across finance, education, startup formation, and enterprise-agent tracks. It evolved from a doc-first startup exercise into a technical-investability loop with runnable demos, tests, evals, receipt artifacts, buyer-facing product surfaces, Taste Bank calibration, and buyer usefulness review.

The operating law became:

> Product surface + receipt + investment readiness beats code volume or narrative polish.

The later rounds moved the strongest repos into standalone public product repos, required `make demo`, `make test`, `make eval`, and `make receipt`, and forced each product to disclose mocks and remaining investment blockers.

## Buyer-Compelling Outcome

The buyer judge found 19 repos compelling enough to justify continued attention, but all 19 were classified as `external-proof-required`. That means the repos had credible product shape and useful artifacts, but did not yet have investment-grade customer or market evidence.

The remaining non-compelling repo, Redo, is held and should not receive independent arena compute.

## Proof-Now Finalist Outcome

The external-proof ranking selected five repos as the fastest possible reality-contact candidates:

1. CanonKit
2. Wedgewise
3. ProofPulse
4. PrepPilot
5. SignalDraft

These are not winners. They are the repos most worth revisiting if a real customer, real data source, or real use case appears.

## Final Decision

No fictional $10M seed check is awarded.

The portfolio proved that Codex can produce serious product-shaped repos under a strict validator. It did not prove that any company deserves investment. The missing bar is external evidence: a real target user must see the product, understand it, and want the output.

The arena is closed unless that evidence appears for a specific repo.
