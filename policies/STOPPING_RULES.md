# Stopping Rules

This is a Demo Day arena, not an endless build environment.

Each company must stop developing when it has enough product, narrative, and evidence for an investor to make a yes/no funding decision.

Development may continue only if the next task materially improves one of:

1. the core product aha moment,
2. the demo reliability,
3. the customer/wedge clarity,
4. the investment narrative,
5. the evidence supporting fundability.

Development must stop if:

1. the MVP demo works,
2. the standard product contract passes,
3. the eval/receipt evidence is enough for diligence,
4. the final show-not-tell video can be produced from real product capture,
5. further work is mostly cosmetic,
6. the company has a fatal strategic flaw,
7. the next task would not change the funding decision.

The goal is not to build a complete product.

The goal is to build enough of a company to decide whether it deserves the $10M check.

## Show-Not-Tell Video Gate

The final voiced product walkthrough is not a development shortcut.

A team may stop product development and enter video production only after the product has already cleared technical diligence, executable eval/verification evidence, mock honesty, secret safety, and investor-readiness thresholds.

Before that point, the next task must be product work, not video work.

## Anti-Generic Gate

Before building, each team must answer:

> Why is this not just "ChatGPT for X"?

If the answer is weak, develop the thesis before writing more code.

## Feature Freeze Rule

After the MVP produces the core aha moment, all further work must improve one of:

- demo clarity,
- investment credibility,
- customer specificity,
- evidence quality,
- revenue logic.

If a task does not improve one of those, do not do it.
