# Anti-Pattern: Pretty But Not Useful

## What It Looks Like

The product has polished visuals, motion, or screenshots, but the output does not save time, improve judgment, reduce risk, or help the target buyer act.

## Why It Fails

Visual polish can make weak workflows look better for a minute, but it does not create buyer pull. Serious product taste means usefulness under the polish.

## How To Detect It

- The first impression is strong but the artifact is shallow.
- The UI has decorative cards, metrics, or charts unsupported by real logic.
- The demo relies on aesthetics more than workflow advantage.
- A buyer would not know what to do with the output.

## How To Fix It

Start from the buyer artifact. Make the product produce something reviewable, editable, forwardable, or decision-useful. Then polish the surface around that workflow.
