# Reference Profile: RPInSight

## Why This Is In The Taste Bank

RPInSight is a taste anchor for polished insight products: interfaces that turn raw material into decision-ready understanding. Use it as a standard for product completeness, information hierarchy, and buyer-facing clarity.

## Product Surface

The bar to borrow is an insight workspace rather than a document dump. The surface should expose inputs, intermediate reasoning, key findings, review states, and next actions.

## Workflow Clarity

RPInSight-style quality means the product is organized around how an operator actually makes a decision. The UI should not feel like a generic dashboard; it should feel like a workflow with a specific job to be done.

## Demo Flow

The demo should show a user moving from unstructured or scattered context to a ranked, editable, decision-useful artifact. The viewer should understand what became easier and why the output matters.

## Repo Quality

Borrow the expectation that a serious product repo has clear boundaries between interface, data handling, domain logic, and evaluation. A product that can only be understood by reading one script is below the bar.

## Proof / Evaluation Quality

The strongest proof is not a flashy screen. It is evidence that outputs are grounded in inputs, that important fields can be checked, and that the product improves a concrete decision workflow.

## What To Copy

Standards only:

- Polished decision-oriented information hierarchy
- Reviewable generated artifacts
- Product flow that matches a real operator's day
- Clear separation between product surface and engine
- Evidence that outputs are grounded, not pasted

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy the product idea.
- Do not copy branding.
- Do not copy UI layout blindly.
- Do not reuse code unless explicitly permitted.
- Do not treat insight cards as a substitute for workflow.

## Calibration Questions

1. What would make a new repo feel weaker than this?
2. What does this repo prove quickly?
3. What is the closest equivalent of its aha moment for the current product?
