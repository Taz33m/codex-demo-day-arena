# Reference Profile: NarrativeDesk

## Why This Is In The Taste Bank

NarrativeDesk is a taste anchor for products that make complex narrative work replayable, inspectable, and evaluable. It sets a bar for turning analysis into a workbench rather than a one-shot report.

## Product Surface

The bar to borrow is a workbench surface: inputs, timelines, generated narrative artifacts, comparison states, and review controls. The surface should help the user understand change over time, not just read a final answer.

## Workflow Clarity

NarrativeDesk-style quality means the product is built around a repeatable analytical workflow. A user should understand where evidence enters, how the product transforms it, and what artifact leaves the system.

## Demo Flow

A strong equivalent demo shows a concrete before-and-after: raw narrative material goes in, the product organizes or replays it, and the user receives a buyer-relevant artifact they can inspect or act on.

## Repo Quality

Borrow the workbench discipline: deterministic demo paths, fixtures or sample inputs, generated outputs, clear command surface, and evaluation hooks that make product behavior visible.

## Proof / Evaluation Quality

The proof standard is replayability. If a judge reruns the same scenario, they should see consistent outputs and understand which parts are generated, mocked, or evaluated.

## What To Copy

Standards only:

- Workbench framing for complex knowledge work
- Generated artifacts with inspectable provenance
- Replayable demo scenarios
- Evaluation that checks useful output properties
- Honest distinction between mocked and real behavior

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy the product idea.
- Do not copy branding.
- Do not copy UI layout blindly.
- Do not reuse code unless explicitly permitted.
- Do not mistake a generated report for a product workbench.

## Calibration Questions

1. What would make a new repo feel weaker than this?
2. What does this repo prove quickly?
3. What is the closest equivalent of its aha moment for the current product?
