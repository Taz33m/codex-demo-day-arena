# Reference Profile: Citadail

## Why This Is In The Taste Bank

Citadail is a taste anchor for ambitious, operator-grade financial software. It sets a bar for dense product surfaces, serious workflow framing, and a product that feels like it wants to own a high-value job rather than wrap a model call.

## Product Surface

The bar to borrow is a cockpit-like surface: state, decisions, sources, generated work, and operator controls in one place. A comparable product should make the user's next action obvious.

## Workflow Clarity

Citadail-style quality means the workflow is legible quickly: signal enters, judgment is formed, risk is checked, and an actionable artifact emerges. The user should not need to infer what job the product is for.

## Demo Flow

A strong equivalent demo starts from messy, high-friction inputs and reaches a decision-quality output within a minute. The demo should show the product doing work, not merely describing what it could do.

## Repo Quality

Borrow the seriousness: clear app structure, product surface code, domain logic, repeatable commands, and enough test/eval evidence to show this is not a static mock.

## Proof / Evaluation Quality

Financial products need traceable reasoning, source visibility, risk controls, and repeatable outputs. The important standard is not perfection; it is credible proof that the product's output is generated, inspectable, and defensible.

## What To Copy

Standards only:

- Dense operator-facing product surface
- Specific high-value workflow
- Visible state and decision context
- Aha moment tied to a real buyer artifact
- Technical credibility beneath the surface

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy the product idea.
- Do not copy branding.
- Do not copy UI layout blindly.
- Do not reuse code unless explicitly permitted.
- Do not collapse into the same domain unless assigned.

## Calibration Questions

1. What would make a new repo feel weaker than this?
2. What does this repo prove quickly?
3. What is the closest equivalent of its aha moment for the current product?
